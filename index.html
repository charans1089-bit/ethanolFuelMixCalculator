Dyno data demonstrating the absolute flow limit of the stock FA24 high-pressure fuel pump. Pushing past E50 daily risks HPFP failure.

💡Low Fuel Light
When the yellow low-fuel light hits, the needle rests on E — but Subaru builds in a safety reserve of ~2.0–2.5 gallons still in tank. Fill immediately and you'll only fit about 14–14.5 gallons total.
⛽Top-Off Rule
Always pump E85 first per the chart, then switch to the 93 pump and fill until the handle clicks off automatically. This gives the most accurate final blend.
🔄First Mix Warning
If your tank is currently full of pure 93, your first fill will land around ~E35 because existing fuel dilutes the mix. By the 2nd or 3rd fillup you'll stabilize at E45.
DISCLAIMER & LIABILITY: This calculator relies on mathematical estimates assuming exactly 85% ethanol in pump E85 and 10% ethanol in 93 Premium. Real-world pump E85 fluctuates heavily by season and state (often E51–E83). Due to this, your final mix will vary. Always monitor your final ethanol percentage directly via your Cobb Accessport.
